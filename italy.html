Overview: The most vibrant and bizarre culture may be found in Italy. After all, the Roman Empire founded and the Roman Catholic Church preserved this country as the center of European civilisation. You'll come face-to-face with some of the most famous sights from this country's 2,000-year history as you travel through Italy, including the playful Trevi Fountain and the ancient Colosseum in Rome, the Leaning Tower in Pisa, the Renaissance masterpieces of Michelangelo's David and Botticelli's Venus in Florence, and the island city of elegant decay known as Venice. Italy provides Europe's richest cultural heritage in addition to these well-known landmarks. In a dynamic, fully modernized nation, traditions still exist. Keep both the historical and contemporary Italy in mind when you go.

Overview: Papua New Guinea is a diverse and fascinating country located in the southwestern Pacific Ocean. With over 800 languages and 600 islands, it is a destination for adventure seekers looking for unique experiences. Visitors can explore dense rainforests, climb active volcanoes, dive among WWII wrecks, visit traditional villages, and witness colorful festivals. It is also home to some of the world's most remote and unspoiled natural landscapes, including the mighty Sepik River, the stunning Kokoda Track, and the pristine beaches of the Milne Bay province. While the country faces some challenges, such as poverty and crime, visitors can still enjoy a safe and rewarding trip by being aware of their surroundings and taking necessary precautions.
